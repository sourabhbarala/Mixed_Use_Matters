<h3 align="center">Abstract</h3>

<p align="justify">
Accurate information about building usage is essential for applications such as disaster response, urban planning, and energy management. However, in most Indian cities, such data is not available at the building level. Although platforms like OpenStreetMap offer free access to building and road geometries, they often lack detailed annotations on building usage. To address this gap, we adopt a Machine Learning approach to classify buildings in Hyderabad, India, into _residential_ and _non-residential_ categories. Our benchmarking results achieve a macro F1-score of 0.85, with class-wise F1-scores of 0.98 for residential and 0.71 for non-residential buildings. Further analysis revealed that the lower performance for the non-residential class is primarily due to the presence of mixed-use buildings and the limited representation of large residential structures in the training data. This study highlights both the opportunities and challenges associated with utilising OSM data for large-scale building usage classification in the Indian context.
</p>
